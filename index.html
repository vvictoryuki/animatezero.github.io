Our Unique Insights and Proposed Zero-Shot Methodology

Video diffusion models (VDMs) have made significant progress in the past year. However, obtaining satisfactory videos using VDMs still requires frequent trial and error, which consumes a large amount of computational resources and time, making it highly inefficient. To address this issue, we propose a step-by-step video generation framework that first generates images (Step1) and then generates videos from the images (Step2). Since image generation is an efficient and highly controllable process, this approach greatly improves the controllability and efficiency of video generation.

In addition, as a key technology module for step-by-step video generation, image-to-video has recently become a highly focused task with numerous related works emerging. However, almost all of these works require specific training. Our unique insight is that we believe the gap between T2V and I2V tasks is small, and satisfactory I2V performance can be achieved through zero-shot modifications on pre-trained T2V models. By introducing spatial appearance control and temporal consistency control, we demonstrate for the first time the potential of VDMs for zero-shot image-to-video generation. This approach will also inspire the architectural design of subsequent training-based I2V works.

Application: Video Editing

AnimateZero can also be used for better video editing, both for generated videos and real videos. One common use of AnimateDiff (AD) is to assist ControlNet (CN) in video editing, but it still has a domain gap problem. AnimateZero (AZ) has obvious advantages in this regard, namely generating videos with higher subjective quality and higher matching degree with the given text prompt.
